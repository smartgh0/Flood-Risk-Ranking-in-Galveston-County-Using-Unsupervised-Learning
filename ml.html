Dimensionality Reduction

Dimensionality reduction techniques applied:

  • PCA (Principal Component Analysis): Initially used, later replaced by t-SNE.
  • t-SNE (t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding): Chosen for its ability to preserve local data structure.

Clustering

Clustering methods used to identify flood risk levels:

  • Elbow Method: Applied to determine the optimal number of clusters (k=4).
  • K-Means Clustering: Used to group zip codes into four clusters representing different flood risk levels.

Visualization

Visualizations created to present the clustering results:

  • Interactive 2D and 3D scatter plots to visualize the clusters.
  • Geospatial visualization mapping flood risk levels across Galveston County.
